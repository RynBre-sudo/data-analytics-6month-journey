# Monthly Plan Review Guide
## How to Adjust Your 6-Month Data Analytics Plan as You Learn

---

## Why Monthly Reviews Matter

Your 6-month plan is a **guide, not a prison**. As you learn and build real projects, you'll discover:
- What you genuinely enjoy
- What topics resonate with you
- What's easier/harder than expected
- Where you want to focus more

**Monthly reviews let you adapt the plan to fit YOU—not force yourself to fit the plan.**

---

## Monthly Review Process

### At the End of Each Month:

**1. Start a new chat with Claude**
   - Fresh context for each month's reflection
   - Clear delineation between months
   - Easy to track progress over time

**2. Share your reflections:**

**What went well?**
   - Which projects did you enjoy most?
   - What surprised you (in a good way)?
   - What felt easier than expected?
   - What are you proud of?

**What was harder than expected?**
   - Which topics took longer?
   - Where did you get stuck?
   - What concepts confused you?
   - What frustrated you?

**Did you enjoy the specialization path you chose?**
   - Are you excited about Customer Analytics or BI?
   - Do you want to explore the other one more?
   - Did a third area (like Product Analytics) catch your interest?
   - Are both equally appealing?

**Any topics that surprised you?**
   - Something you expected to hate but loved?
   - Something you thought would be easy but was hard?
   - New interests that emerged?
   - Skills that matter more than you thought?

**Anything you want to pivot on?**
   - Want to swap Month 3 focus?
   - Need more foundational work?
   - Want to go deeper in one area?
   - Ready to move faster/slower?

---

## Then Discuss Adjustments

**Examples of adjustments you might make:**

**Scenario 1: You love Customer Analytics, hate BI**
- Could skip Month 3 BI deep dive
- Focus more on advanced customer analytics in Month 5
- Still explore BI basics (knowledge is good) but lighter
- Result: Specialize deeper in CA instead of dual specialization

**Scenario 2: You love BI, Customer Analytics is boring**
- Accelerate through Month 2 (minimum projects)
- Go deeper in Month 3 BI work
- Do more BI advanced projects in Month 5
- Result: BI specialist with CA foundation

**Scenario 3: Month 1 fundamentals took longer**
- No problem—extend Month 1 to 6 weeks
- Start Month 2 when YOU'RE ready, not on schedule
- Adjust other months accordingly
- Result: Deeper foundation, longer overall timeline (7 months instead of 6)

**Scenario 4: You want to add Product Analytics**
- Already interested from Month 5?
- Could do CA + BI + Product Analytics (3 specializations)
- Means extending to 7-8 months
- Or picking which two matter most

**Scenario 5: You're progressing faster than expected**
- Some people fly through Month 1
- Could add extra projects
- Could deepen existing projects
- Could explore advanced topics earlier

**Opening:**
```

GitHub/Work: [Links to projects]

What went well: [What you enjoyed/succeeded at]

What was harder: [What took longer/was confusing]

Feedback: [What surprised you, what you want to adjust]

Questions for you:
1. [Specific question about adjusting plan]
2. [Specific question about next month]
```

**Example:**
```
Month 2 Review: Customer Analytics Deep Dive

Summary: Completed Reforge CA course + 3 projects (cohorts, LTV, churn)

GitHub: 
- Cohort analysis: https://github.com/brandy/customer-analytics-project-1
- LTV segmentation: https://github.com/brandy/customer-analytics-project-2
- Churn prediction: https://github.com/brandy/customer-analytics-project-3

What went well: 
- Cohort analysis was really interesting and I understood it quickly
- Reforge course was excellent, clearer than expected
- Started seeing patterns in data that felt meaningful

What was harder:
- Churn prediction model took longer than expected
- Statistics concepts needed more review
- Building the model in Python was trickier than analysis

Feedback:
- I'm genuinely excited about customer metrics now
- BI still sounds interesting but less appealing
- Would like to go deeper in CA, lighter in BI

Questions for you:
- Should I focus Month 3 on BI or go deeper in CA?
- Is it OK to skip some BI projects and add CA projects instead?
```

## Keys to Making Reviews Work

**Be honest:**
- If something sucked, say so
- If you want to pivot, speak up
- If you're struggling, let me know
- If you're bored, change direction

**Be specific:**
- Show actual work (GitHub, dashboards)
- Give examples of what was hard
- Name the topics that excited you
- Share actual time/effort spent

**Be open to suggestions:**
- I might see patterns you don't
- Small adjustments can help a lot
- Trying something different is OK
- There's usually a good compromise

**Keep momentum:**
- Review within 1-2 weeks of month end
- Don't let a month pass without checking in
- Adjust quickly (don't wait for next month to fix)
- Celebrate wins while adjusting

---

## What Happens If You Don't Adjust?

**It's totally fine.**

If Month 1 goes great and Month 2 goes great and you love the whole plan → stick with it! No need to review.

**But reviews help if:**
- You're struggling
- You're bored
- You discovered a new passion
- Pacing isn't working
- You want to go deeper

**Think of them as optional checkpoints, not required bureaucracy.**

---

## By Month 6, Your Plan Might Look Different

That's actually a GOOD sign.

**Month 1 Plan:** Customer Analytics + BI equally
**Month 2 (after review):** Customer Analytics more, BI lighter
**Month 3 (after review):** CA + Product Analytics bonus
**Month 4 (after review):** Two CA specializations + light BI
**Month 5 (after review):** Advanced CA, skip advanced BI
**Month 6 (after review):** Polish CA portfolio, 1-2 BI projects for breadth

**Result:** You became a CA specialist (which you discovered you love) instead of generic dual-specialist.

That's better than forcing yourself through a generic plan.
